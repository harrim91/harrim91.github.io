About

Problem Solver

A system is only as good as the sum of its parts. A key to solving any problem is the ability to disect a system and understand what each of those parts should do.

My undergrad and postgrad studies in Mathematics and Philosophy, two years as a software tester, and my training at Makers Academy have shaped the way I think and given me a strong ability to break problems down, and to quickly grasp and manipulate complex ideas to diagnose and solve problems.

Maker

For me, creativity is the ability to experiment. It's the ability to take things apart, understand what each part does, and then to see how you could put those parts together in a new, different order.

I love understanding how things work, and I love making things - not just coding, I also make music and wooden spoons in my spare time. My ultimate dream is to make a clock.

Craftsman

At Makers Academy, I had best practices and XP principles drilled into me from the start. I am always keen to try new things and getting stuck doesn't phase me - I see challenges as opportunities to broaden my understanding.

I am at the start of my craftsmanship journey, and as such try to participate in the software community as much as possible. I attend meetups and contribute to open-source projects, as well as regularly coaching at Codebar.
